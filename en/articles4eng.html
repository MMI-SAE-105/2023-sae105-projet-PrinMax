"Shining" by Stephen King: A psychological horror book"

Published in 1977, "Shining" by Stephen King is become a classic of horror literature, a novel that has terrified generations of readers.

The cover of the shining book.

This book plunges into the madness of the mind human, offering an unforgettable experience. The Story: "Shining" tells the story of Jack Torrance, a writer and former teacher, who accepts a employment as a caretaker of the Overlook Hotel, a secluded hotel in the Colorado Mountains. The position of guardian means that he, his wife Wendy and their young son Danny will be alone in the hotel throughout the winter season, isolated and away from the rest of the world. Jack hopes take advantage of this solitude to write, but rapidly, supernatural forces and evil begin to manifest, affecting Jack’s mental health and putting his family at risk.

The influence of "Shining":

One of the peculiarities of "Shining" lies in the psychic gift of Danny, Jack’s son. He has the "Shining," a telepathic power which allows him to see the past, the present and the future. He can also feel emotions others and communicate mentally with people who also have that power. The "Shining" is both a blessing and a curse, for it makes him sensitive to the forces malefic hotel. The Overlook Hotel, as the central location of history, comes to life in a sinister way. It becomes a character in itself, haunted by a dark past and traumatic events. Empty corridors, abandoned rooms and the snow-covered labyrinth that surrounds the hotel create an oppressive, nightmarish atmosphere and threatening to the protagonists, the diving into a scary reality.

The Fight Against Madness:

The novel explores the themes of isolation and madness. As the hotel reaches and attacks the mental fragility of Jack, Wendy and Danny, struggle to escape the grip of evil that surrounds in the hotel. Psychological tension is palpable throughout the book, making this delivers more than just a horror story. It’s a deep dive into the human soul and his inner demons like madness.

In conclusion:

"Shining" by Stephen King is a masterpiece of literary horror, a book that continues to haunt the minds of readers for decades after its publication. It explores the darker human beings and horrors that can arise from loneliness and madness. It is a pillier of horror literature and a work that deserves its place in culture popular. "Shining" is an experience terrifying and unforgettable, which leaves a mark indelible in the minds of those who read it.
